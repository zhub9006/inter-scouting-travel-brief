## 2. Milan City Profile & History

### Overview
Milan is the regional capital of Lombardy and Italy's second-most populous city (1.36M in the city proper; 6.5M metro). It is the country's economic capital, accounting for ~20% of Italy's GDP.

### Key Historical Milestones
- **~590 BC** — Founded by Celtic Insubres tribe as *Medhelanon*
- **222 BC** — Conquered by Rome, renamed *Mediolanum*
- **Late 4th century** — Briefly served as capital of the Western Roman Empire; Emperor Constantine issued the Edict of Milan (313 AD), granting religious tolerance
- **1395** — Gian Galeazzo Visconti became first Duke of Milan
- **1450** — Francesco Sforza conquered Milan; the Sforza dynasty attracted Leonardo da Vinci and Bramante, making Milan a Renaissance powerhouse
- **1535–1706** — Spanish Habsburg rule; the Great Plague of 1629–31 killed ~60,000 (described in Manzoni's *The Betrothed*)
- **1706–1796** — Austrian Habsburg rule; Empress Maria Theresa renovated the city, founding Teatro alla Scala (1778)
- **1848** — The "Five Days" rebellion against Austrian rule
- **1861** — Incorporated into the unified Kingdom of Italy
- **Post-WWII** — Italian economic miracle; population peaked at 1.74M in 1973
- **1980s** — Became a global fashion capital (Armani, Prada, Versace, Dolce & Gabbana)
- **21st century** — Major urban renewal (Porta Nuova, CityLife districts); hosted Expo 2015 and Winter Olympics 2026

### Architecture Highlights
- **Milan Cathedral (Duomo)** — Largest church in Italy, built 1386–1877
- **Teatro alla Scala** — One of the world's most prestigious opera houses
- **Castello Sforzesco** — Renaissance fortress housing multiple museums
- **Santa Maria delle Grazie** — Home of Leonardo's *The Last Supper*
- **Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II** — World's oldest shopping mall (1865–77)
- **Monte Stella** — Artificial hill near San Siro built from WWII bombing debris; offers panoramic city views

## 3. Local Culture & Traditions

### Milanese Cuisine
Milan's culinary tradition is distinctively Northern Italian — more rice than pasta, more butter than olive oil, and very little tomato or fish.

**Must-try dishes:**
- **Risotto alla Milanese** — Saffron-infused risotto with beef marrow
- **Cotoletta alla Milanese** — Breaded veal cutlet pan-fried in butter (similar to Wiener Schnitzel)
- **Ossobuco** — Braised veal shank with gremolata
- **Cassoeula** — Stewed pork ribs & sausage with Savoy cabbage (winter dish)
- **Busecca** — Stewed tripe with beans
- **Mondeghili** — Fried meatballs made from leftover meat

**Seasonal pastries:**
- **Panettone** — Christmas sweet bread (Milan's gift to the world)
- **Colomba** — Easter dove-shaped glazed cake
- **Chiacchiere** — Carnival fritters
- **Pane dei Morti** — All Souls' Day cinnamon cookies

**Local cheeses:** Gorgonzola, Mascarpone, Taleggio, Quartirolo
**Local wine:** San Colombano DOC (red, from ~40km SE of Milan)

### Cultural Traditions
- **Ambrosian Rite** — Milan uses its own Catholic liturgy (different from Roman Rite); Lent starts 4 days later
- **La Scala Season Opening** — December 7th (Feast of St. Ambrose), a major social event
- **Festa di Sant'Ambrogio** — December 7th, Milan's patron saint; local holiday with the Oh Bej! Oh Bej! street market
- **Sciura culture** — The elegant, fashionable Milanese women who embody the city's style
- **Aperitivo** — Milan's beloved pre-dinner drink and snacks culture (born here!); the Navigli district is the aperitivo hotspot
- **Football passion** — The Derby della Madonnina (AC Milan vs Inter) is one of the world's great rivalries

### Practical Cultural Tips
- Greetings: A firm handshake; close friends kiss both cheeks
- Dress: Milan is fashion-conscious — smart casual is the minimum, even for match day
- Tipping: Not obligatory; rounding up the bill is appreciated
- Language: Italian; the Milanese dialect is still spoken by some locals
- Business hours: Shops often close 1–3:30 PM for riposo; restaurants may close between lunch and dinner

- **Fuel stations nearby**: Tamoil (Via Vodice), Q8 (Viale Renato Serra), Esso (Via Palatino), IP (Via Simone Stratico), Total (Via Novara area)

- **Area C congestion charge**: Central Milan requires a €7.50 daily entry fee for vehicles

### Getting to San Siro from Key Locations

**From Milano Centrale (Main Train Station):**
- Take M2 (Green) → Cadorna → M1 (Red) → Lotto → M5 (Lilac) → San Siro Stadio (~30 min)
- Or: Take M3 (Yellow) → Duomo → M1 (Red) → Lotto → M5 → San Siro Stadio (~35 min)

**From Linate Airport:**
- M4 (Blue) metro from airport → San Babila → M1 → Lotto → M5 → San Siro Stadio (~50 min)

**From Malpensa Airport:**
- Malpensa Express train → Cadorna → M1 → Lotto → M5 → San Siro Stadio (~65 min)

**From Bergamo (Orio al Serio) Airport:**
- Bus to Bergamo station → train to Milano Centrale → metro as above (~90 min)

### ATM Transit Tips
- Single ticket: €2.20 (valid 90 min on metro, tram, bus within urban area)
- Day pass: €7.60
- Buy tickets at metro stations, tabacchi, or via ATM Mobile app
- Match days: Extra metro/tram services run to San Siro; expect crowds
